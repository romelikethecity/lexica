Real Estate Attorney Data

Verified contacts for commercial and residential real estate attorneys, title counsel, land use lawyers, and development attorneys.

What's included

Lexica's real estate dataset covers every actively licensed attorney practicing in this area across all 50 states. Each record includes the attorney's name, verified email address, direct phone number, firm name and address, state bar number with active status confirmation, and practice area classification.

Data is sourced from state bar associations and cross-referenced against court records, business filings, and firm websites. Every contact is deliverability-tested before inclusion in your list.

Who uses this data

Teams selling legal technology, recruiting services, court reporting, expert witness placement, marketing services, and e-discovery tools to real estate attorneys and their firms. If your product or service serves this segment of the legal market, Lexica provides the contacts you need to reach decision-makers directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What real estate attorney data fields are included?

Standard fields: attorney name, email (deliverability-tested), direct phone, firm name and address, state bar number, active/inactive status, and practice area classification. We can add firm size data or technology detection for an additional fee.

How is the data verified?

Every contact starts with a state bar record. We verify the attorney is actively licensed, confirm their firm affiliation, validate their email address, and check their phone number. Records that fail any verification step are flagged or excluded.

How fresh is the data?

Each list is built to order using the most current bar records available at the time. We don't recycle old data. Email and phone verification happens in the final 48 hours before delivery, so contact accuracy reflects current status.

Do you cover real estate attorneys in all states?

We cover every US jurisdiction. Each state bar publishes attorney records on its own schedule. We maintain pipelines for all 50 states and adjust our sourcing to match each bar's data refresh cycle.
